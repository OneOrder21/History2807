Period of devastation(Early 1900s)

Historical Hurricane Data

Jamaica endured a devastating series of hurricanes in the early 20th century that left lasting impacts. Between 1903 and 1939, the island was struck by at least eight major storms, including: The catastrophic 1903 Category 3 hurricane (120 mph winds, 65-90 deaths, $10M damage) Multiple deadly storms in the 1910s that killed hundreds collectively Repeated destruction of infrastructure and plantations Frequent flooding from torrential rains These hurricanes caused: • Significant loss of life (dozens to hundreds per major storm) • Massive economic damage (millions in contemporary dollars) • Long-term recovery challenges • Demonstrated Jamaica's geographic vulnerability The period highlights how tropical cyclones have historically shaped Jamaica's development and underscores the ongoing climate challenges facing Caribbean nations.
